## Abstract

This research note reads Dmitruk et al.'s PLOS Computational Biology paper on persistent homology, gaze, EEG, and abstract art as a guarded external readout surface for Book VII's categorical aesthetics. It does not claim that topology measures beauty or that the external paper validates Category tau. Instead, it builds a typed bridge from image-filtration descriptors to possible pre-symbolic motif readout, while making the lossy measurement chain, caveats, and falsification surfaces explicit.

## Claim Boundary

Core claim: Dmitruk et al. provide a promising empirical descriptor family for visual-composition readout: image to filtration descriptor to feature map to viewer trace to Book VII interpretation. Every arrow in that chain is lossy, and the note's contribution is a typed bridge protocol rather than a new empirical result.

Does not claim:
- It does not claim that persistent homology measures beauty.
- It does not claim that Dmitruk et al. prove or validate Category tau.
- It does not claim that image-filtration topology is Book VII topology.
- It does not claim proof of artist intent, universal composition law, deployment relevance, policy adoption, or achieved impact.

Falsification surface:
- Independent reproduction fails to recover the reported image-topology descriptors or gaze/topology comparisons.
- Color-aware topology, controlled synthesis, saliency baselines, or curatorial controls explain away the Book VII-facing bridge value.
- Preregistered Category-tau-facing mappings from image descriptors to motif quantities fail under external review or future empirical testing.
